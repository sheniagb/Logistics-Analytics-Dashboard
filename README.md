# 📊 Logistics Performance Dashboard (Power BI)

##  Overview  
I worked on this project to analyse how a logistics company has been performing over time, specifically between 2020 and 2024.

The business was growing, but behind the scenes there were clear issues:
- Too many shipments stuck “in progress”  
- Delays affecting important deliveries  
- Some transport methods clearly underperforming  
- Customer satisfaction not where it should be  

So the goal here wasn’t just to build a dashboard but to figure out what’s actually going wrong and where improvements can be made.

---

##  The Problem  
At a high level, the company didn’t have clear visibility into its operations.

Different teams were looking at different things, but no one had a full picture of:
- What’s being delivered on time  
- What’s delayed  
- Which transport methods are reliable  
- Why customers are unhappy  

That’s what this project solves.

---

##  What I Focused On  

I structured the analysis around 3 key areas:

### 1. Shipment Performance  
- How many shipments are actually being completed?  
- How many are stuck in progress?  
- Are things improving over time?

---

### 2. Transport & Regional Breakdown  
- Which transport modes perform best (Jet, Bus, Lorry, Motorbike)?  
- Where are the delays coming from?  
- Which cities are under pressure?

---

### 3. Customer Experience  
- What’s driving satisfaction scores?  
- Does delivery time affect ratings? (Spoiler: it does)  
- Are payment issues affecting customer experience?

---

##  Tools Used  

- Power BI (Dashboard & visuals)  
- Power Query (data cleaning)  
- DAX (calculations & KPIs)  

---

##  What the Data Says (Key Insights)

###  Too Many Shipments Are Stuck  
One of the biggest red flags:
- ~47K shipments are still in progress
- Only ~16.7K are completed

That’s a huge gap and clearly shows a process or capacity issue.

---

### 🚚 Some Transport Modes Are Slowing Everything Down  
- Motorbikes have the slowest delivery time (~10.4 days)  
- Lorries and Jets aren’t much better (~9.9 days)  
- Buses seem to perform slightly better  

This suggests the business isn’t aligning transport type with urgency properly.

---

###  High-Priority Shipments Aren’t Being Treated Like Priority  
You’d expect urgent deliveries to be the fastest — but that’s not always happening.

This points to:
- Poor allocation of transport  
- Lack of SLA enforcement  

---

### 🌍 Regional Imbalance  
- Lagos carries the highest shipment volume (no surprise)  
- But it also shows signs of delivery pressure and delays  

This tells me routes and capacity aren’t evenly optimised.

---

### ⭐ Customer Satisfaction Is Mid (At Best)  
Average satisfaction sits around 5.5 / 10

And the pattern is clear:
> The longer the delivery time → the worse the rating

---

### 💳 Payments Are a Hidden Problem  
A large chunk of shipments are:
- Pending  
- Overdue  

This likely adds friction to the customer experience and impacts satisfaction.

---

## 📈 Why This Matters  

This dashboard helps the business:
- Spot bottlenecks quickly  
- Understand which transport modes to trust  
- Improve delivery timelines  
- Make better operational decisions  

---

##  My Recommendations  

If I were advising the business directly:

- Prioritise high-value & urgent shipments properly  
- Reduce reliance on slower transport modes for critical deliveries  
- Optimise routes in high-volume areas like Lagos  
- Introduce SLA tracking for delivery performance  
- Clean up payment processes to improve customer experience

- Operational problems usually show up clearly when you look at the data properly
